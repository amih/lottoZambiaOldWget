TOP 5 Places in Africa to Visit When you Win the Zambian Lotto

Here are the favorite spots where you can escape reality for a while when you win the Zambian Lotto. Enjoy the majestic wildlife, crystal blue beaches, and rich African culture. So without further ado, let’s dive in.

 

Rwanda - The Land of a Thousand Hills

Hands down, Rwanda has one of the most stunning sceneries in Africa. The country features a breathtaking mountainous landscape and is the home to six active volcanoes. Your visit to this country wouldn't be complete without exploring the Volcanoes National Park. The main attraction here is the mountain gorilla trek which gives you the chance to encounter the mountain gorillas and golden monkeys in their natural habitat.

Also, you can explore further to Lake Kivu, which is the sixth-largest lake in Africa. Enjoy a wide range of activities here like kayaking, boat tours, and fishing. One of the best places to spend the night in Rwanda would definitely be Mountain Gorilla View Lodge which is closer to the Volcanoes National Park. Besides that, you can consider the Gorillas Lake Kivu Hotel, which will give you the ultimate feeling of luxury.

South Africa

One of the best places in Africa that knows just how to let you enjoy luxury is South Africa. Most people think South Africa is only an ideal travel destination for Western visitors because of the infrastructure and large cities. However, South Africa is just the ideal destination when you win the Zambian Lotto. It will give you a taste of different cultures enjoying all the perks of the lush urban life. Cape Town overlooks the sea and features some of the most breathtaking beaches.

If you love a little bit of history, be sure to visit Robben Island, where freedom fighter Nelson Mandela was imprisoned. The iSimangaliso Wetlands Park in KwaZulu-Natal is known for its natural beauty. The park is home to Africa's largest estuarine system, which is a fascinating fact. You'll trek among historic coastal dunes and beaches that reach all the way to Mozambique's Kosi Bay. The Western Cape offers a diverse range of activities. You may relax and enjoy the picturesque Garden Route, home to a diverse range of vegetation. Many additional hidden gems can be found along the way, including true South African culture and cuisine.

Tanzania

Tanzania is the ideal destination if you want to enjoy a nice classical African Safari. Along with Kenya, Tanzania is one of the original African safari destinations. In Tanzania, you will enjoy sports, high wildlife concentration, and the African plains' iconic scenery. Tanzania has an extensive tourism infrastructure; therefore, you will enjoy a well-established wildlife safari.

Make sure you experience the Nothern Safari Circuit, which is among the best places for viewing wildlife in Africa. This region hosts several of the superb safari parks, the Serengeti Park and Ngorongoro crater. All these areas are rich in wildlife and big game like buffalos, rhinos, and lions. Also, you can get to witness the infamous wildebeest migration.

Namibia

Namibia is one of the best countries to visit in Africa. Namibia is an extraordinary safari destination that even appears on CNN's list of the best places on earth. This safe Southern African country will offer you outstanding wildlife viewing in Etosha as well as the surreal desert scenery.

The fact that Namibia also has the lowest population on earth is just the ideal place to escape if you are trying to escape the crowds. One of the best destinations that you shouldn't miss on your bucket list is the Namib desert. Here you can escape the rush of modernity as you soak up the beautiful night skies.

The Fish River Canyon is another natural wonder in Namibia. This spectacular canyon is Africa's largest and one of Namibia's most popular tourist destinations. Its massive valley contains the country's longest interior river, and hot springs can be discovered near its lower reaches.

Kenya

Kenya is considered one of the best African tourist countries. The Maasai Mara National Reserve especially always gets a steady influx of visitors throughout the year. Maasai Mara is a jaw-dropping destination where you will find some large African fauna, including lions and elephants, in their natural habitat. If you are looking for a top-notch wild experience, Maasai Mara will give you just that with picture-perfect scenery and an abundance of fun activities.

Aside from that, you can go to Malindi and Watamu to see the diverse marine life that lives in the coral reefs. At Lake Nakuru, you can also go bird watching. And if you adore wildlife, the Amboseli National Park offers a breathtaking view of Mount Kilimanjaro. Furthermore, the Gede Ruins in Watamu will give you with a plethora of information about the island's past if you are interested in culture and history.
