What is a raffle?

A raffle is a type of contest where players buy tickets expecting to win a prize from their tickets. After the tickets have been sold, a draw is conducted to determine the tickets that hold the winning number. You can check today’s lotto results and winning numbers online to see if your ticket has the winning numbers.
It is one of the simplest and fairest forms of lottery games. This is why the game is extremely popular in the continent of Africa.

 

History of raffle lottery games

The history of the raffle lottery is rich as it has always been there in different forms. The name might have been different but the concept of the raffle has always been the same.

Take the example of the Old Testament where Moses used a raffle system to award land to his people. Originally, raffle games were as simple as dice games where players just toss numbers around expecting theirs to be the winning numbers.

The widow of Flemish painter Jan Van Eyck was among the first people to run a recorded raffle. She sold her late husband’s paintings using raffle tickets. Afterward, the idea of printing raffles to raise funds for big projects became an instant idea for many people. Belgium was among the first countries that formalized raffles in the late 1460s.

In the 1500s, raffles spread to Europe before moving to America. Modern raffles started in 1612 in North America.

In Zambia, church groups, community organizations, service clubs, and many more use raffles to raise funds.
