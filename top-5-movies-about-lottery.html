Top 5 Movies about Lottery

The chances of winning the lottery are the pretty thing but you can be the next lucky one to hit the jackpot. The lottery is a real thing and there are many people around the world who have changed their lives by winning the lotteries. Hollywood has also made special movies about lotteries where one ticket changes the lives of the winners.

Some people started taking part in the lottery after watching the online advertisement of films. Different people have different reasons to play the lottery. If you believe in luck, make sure to check the most popular lotto numbers in the world. Here is the list of the top 5 movies about the Lottery.

 

1. Lucky Numbers

The story focuses on J.P. Beaumont, a man who is down on his luck and ends up in big trouble because he loses everything he worked hard for and has nothing left to lose. While in jail, his last hope is to find his sister's winning lottery ticket which contains all the numbers of the winning ticket, and reunite him with his former colleague. The story also shows how one ticket can change your life forever.

2. Brewster's Millions

Brewster's Millions is a 1991 comedy film directed by Penny Marshall, starring Danny DeVito and cross-dressing mannequin Arnold Schwarzenegger. It is based on the eponymous novel by George Barr, Jr., one of the most popular books in the United States during the 1980s. This film also talks about that once you are on a lucky streak in life, you have to think twice before you act which throughout history has been proven to be true.

3. It could happen to you

It Could Happen To You is a 1994 drama film directed by Sidney Lumet and stars Chevy Chase, Kim Cattrall, and Ben Stiller. It tells the story of three friends who win the lottery and what happens to each of them afterward. This film talks about how you should always remember your past in order for you not to repeat it.

4. Lucky

Lucky is a 2011 American comedy-drama film written and directed by John Carroll Lynch. This film talks about how you should have faith in your luck.

5. 29th Street

29th Street is a 1991 drama film directed by Daniel Sullivan. This film tells the story of two men who both get lucky and they have to decide if they will use their luck wisely or not.
